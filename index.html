Key Perspectives

Three Models of Academic Literacy

Study Skills Model: Literacy is viewed as a set of discrete, transferable technical skills, such as grammar, punctuation, and spelling, applicable across contexts.

Academic Socialisation Model: Literacy is seen as contextual and disciplinary, requiring students to adopt the norms, genres, and conventions of specific academic communities.

Academic Literacies Model: Literacy is understood as socially situated and contested, involving negotiation of meaning, identity, and power within academic contexts.

"The academic literacies model recognizes that writing is never just about skills or socialization, but always involves questions of power, identity, and access."

- Lea & Street (2006)

Each model offers different perspectives on how students engage with academic writing demands

Ubuntu Concept

Ubuntu is a philosophical concept that emphasizes our interconnectedness as human beings. "Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu" (I am because we are) captures this worldview focusing on community, mutual caring, and shared humanity.

In academic contexts, Ubuntu encourages collaborative learning, dialectic argumentation, and writing that acknowledges multiple perspectives and the communal nature of knowledge creation.

African Knowledge Systems

Imhotep lived over 4,000 years ago during Egypt's Old Kingdom, specifically around the 27th to 26th century BCE. He held prominent roles in society, serving as chancellor to Pharaoh Djoser and as high priest of the sun god Ra at the sacred city of Heliopolis. His influence extended across both political and religious spheres, making him one of the most revered figures of his time.

Widely recognized as a polymath, Imhotep demonstrated exceptional skill and innovation in multiple fields. As the architect of the Step Pyramid at Saqqara—the first monumental stone structure in history—he revolutionized Egyptian architecture. He was also a pioneering physician, often credited as the founder of Egyptian medicine, with his teachings influencing healing practices for centuries. In addition, he contributed to astronomy and philosophical thought, earning a reputation as a sage and advisor.

"Imhotep was deified after his death as the god of medicine and wisdom, one of the few mortals to achieve divine status in ancient Egypt."
